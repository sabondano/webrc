Officially licensed by:

The Bell UH-1 Huey is a military helicopter powered by a single, turboshaft engine, with a two-bladed main rotor and tail rotor.

The Bell UH-1 Huey was developed by Bell Helicopter to meet the United States Army's requirement for medical evacuation and utility helicopter in 1952, and first flew on 20 October 1956. The original designation "HU-1" led to the helicopter's nickname of Huey. Internal seating is made up of two pilot seats and additional seating for up to 13 passengers in the cabin.
